Why GPL-3.0?¶

We chose the GNU General Public License v3.0 because:

  1. Protects Freedom: Ensures Tourmaline remains free software forever
  2. Encourages Collaboration: Improvements benefit everyone
  3. Prevents Proprietary Lock-in: No one can take it closed-source
  4. Community-Friendly: Compatible with many open-source projects
  5. Well-Established: Widely understood and legally tested

Frequently Asked Questions¶

Can I use Tourmaline in my school or class?¶

Yes! You can freely use Tourmaline for educational purposes at any level.

Can I use Tourmaline in my company?¶

Yes! You can use Tourmaline internally without restrictions. If you distribute software containing Tourmaline, it must be under GPL-3.0.

Can I sell programs written in Tourmaline?¶

Yes! Programs written in Tourmaline are your own work. The GPL applies to Tourmaline itself, not to programs you write with it.

Can I modify Tourmaline for my own use?¶

Yes! You can modify it however you like for personal use without sharing your changes.

Do I need to share my modifications?¶

Only if you distribute them. If you keep modifications private, you don't need to share. But if you give your modified version to others, you must provide the source code under GPL-3.0.

Can I create a closed-source language based on Tourmaline?¶

No. Any derivative work must also be licensed under GPL-3.0 and remain open-source.

Can I use Tourmaline in proprietary software?¶

This depends on how you use it: - Calling Tourmaline as a separate program: Usually okay - Linking/embedding Tourmaline code: Must be GPL-3.0

When in doubt, consult a legal professional.
